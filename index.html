How it works

Simple to learn, hard to master

Each round gives you 5 historical events. Place them on a timeline as accurately as possible.

1
Find the anchors
Identify which event came first and which came last. These define the scale of your timeline for the round.
2
Place the rest
Drag the remaining 3 events onto the bar. A bracket tooltip shows the time window at your cursor as you drag.
3
Score points
Full marks for the right bracket. Partial credit for being close. The precision required scales with the time span.

About

Built for curious minds

History Challenger was built out of a simple frustration: most history quizzes are multiple choice, and multiple choice lets you guess. We wanted something that tested genuine temporal reasoning — not just "did you recognise the name" but "do you actually know when things happened?"

Every game is generated fresh by AI, drawing on the full breadth of human history — from Bronze Age Mesopotamia to the Cold War, from European kingdoms to Asian dynasties, from battles to scientific discoveries to cultural milestones.

The game adapts its scoring to the timeline you're given. Playing events from a single decade? Brackets are 1 year wide. Spanning 3000 years? They're 100 years wide.

Free to play. No account required to start. Sign in to track your scores and streaks over time.

FAQ

Questions

Is it really free?
Yes. You can play without creating an account. Sign in with Google to save your scores, track streaks, and play unlimited games.
How are events generated?
Every game is generated fresh by Claude (Anthropic's AI), drawing on historical knowledge spanning all eras and civilisations. Each session gets unique events you haven't seen before.
Can I choose a historical theme?
Yes — the Theme Challenge mode lets you pick a specific era, region, or topic. Choose from presets like "World War II" or type any custom theme.
What languages are supported?
English, French, German, and Spanish. Select your language on the game screen — all events are generated directly in your chosen language.
How is scoring calculated?
40 points for correctly identifying the earliest and most recent events. 60 points for placing the remaining 3 events accurately. Bracket size and tolerance scale dynamically with your round's time span.
